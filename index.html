A Guide for Growing Engineers

For engineers who’ve shipped code and want to grow the habits, voice, and depth that turn a coder into a teammate a team can rely on.

Two muscles you grow now. Everything else gets built on top.

Explore the Guide

Who This Is For

This guide is for engineers who write working code and close stories — and want more than that. You know your stack enough to get things done. What’s next isn’t more syntax — it’s the habits that separate someone who writes code from someone a team builds around: curiosity (you question, you dig, you ask why) and commitment (you finish, you follow through, your yes means yes).

It sits before two further-along guides. If you already ship reliably and you’re ready to own features end-to-end and lift the team’s craft bar, see the Craft & Ownership companion guide — that’s the next step. If your day-to-day has shifted from building the product to growing the team around you, see Beyond IC. This guide stops before both.

And it’s not a test. There are no grades. The goal is that six months from now you recognise a habit that used to trip you up — and notice you don’t do that anymore.

How to Use It

It’s a reference, not a course. Don’t read it cover to cover. Pick the area that matches what’s actually hard for you right now. Skim the frameworks. Try one thing this week. Come back when the situation shifts.

The self-assessment is there to help you spot your smallest next step — not to rank you. Revisit it every few months; the things that feel hard now should feel routine later.

One framework, actually applied, beats ten frameworks read and forgotten. Small, consistent steps compound.
